Investment thesis

  • Trading at a ~24% discount to peers — 8.1x EV/EBITDA vs a 12.4x peer median, despite the #2 global position and a premium portfolio (Jameson, Absolut, Martell, Chivas).
  • FY26 transition year, growth resuming — Q3 organic net sales +0.1% after a soft H1; guidance +3% to +6% organic p.a. over FY27–29.
  • Clear deleveraging — net debt/EBITDA from ~3.4x to ~2.0x by FY30, on ~80% cash conversion.
  • M&A floors valuation — spirits precedents at ~16.9x median EV/EBITDA imply control-premium value above €108.

Methodology

How every name is valued

Three independent methods, triangulated into one target.

Discounted cash flow

Unlevered FCF discounted at WACC; terminal value cross-checked via Gordon growth and an exit EV/EBITDA multiple. EBIT = Profit from Recurring Operations, taxed at a normalized 25%.

WACC build-up

Cost of equity via CAPM (risk-free, levered beta, ERP), blended with after-tax cost of debt at target structure. Net debt on an IFRS-16 basis.

Trading comparables

EV/EBITDA and EV/Sales against a curated peer set, benchmarked to the peer median to isolate the embedded discount or premium.

Precedent transactions

Control-deal multiples from comparable sector M&A, setting a takeout floor alongside the standalone DCF.

The blended target weights each method, with a two-way sensitivity grid (WACC × terminal assumption) published for every name.
